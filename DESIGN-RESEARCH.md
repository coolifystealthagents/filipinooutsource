# Design research: Philippines staffing homepage

## Search intent

Buyer-intent query: `Philippines outsourcing company staffing`

The page should help a business owner decide what to delegate, how to structure the role, and what to ask before requesting Filipino talent. The offer is limited to talent from the Philippines.

## Five direct organic competitors inspected

### 1. Cloudstaff — cloudstaff.com

The desktop homepage uses a split hero with a large sans-serif headline, a polished employee portrait, pale blue technology graphics, and blue or yellow calls to action. Role cards are easy to scan, and certifications appear early. The cookie panel and newsletter bar obscured part of the hero in the inspected capture. Several later media areas appeared blank or unloaded, and the mix of `Get Pricing`, `Book a meeting`, and discovery-call labels weakens the primary path.

### 2. Emapta — emapta.com

Emapta uses a dark navy hero, bright pink calls to action, a large white headline, and a talent-platform mockup. The short benefit list is direct, but the visual was soft in the inspected capture and the page left a large empty band below the hero. Broad claims such as top-one-percent talent were not supported by visible proof above the fold.

### 3. Outsourced — outsourced.ph

Outsourced leads with a navy and cyan hero, custom line art, a diagonal section transition, and a clear Philippines-specific headline. Four compact benefit cards make the buying case quickly. Its process section and role catalogue are strong, though the homepage is long, several carousels clip at the right edge, and multiple CTA labels compete for attention.

### 4. Remote Staff — remotestaff.com

Remote Staff pairs editorial serif headings with a white, dark green, and lime palette. Its role catalogue and four-part operating process are useful. In the inspected desktop capture, pale hero text sat on a bright office photo, trust marks were faint, and unusually large blank bands made much of the page look unfinished. The calculator drew more attention than the staffing story.

### 5. Staff Domain — staffdomain.com

Staff Domain uses a large rounded cobalt hero, geometric sans-serif type, lime accents, subtle contour lines, and compact certification cards. Its strongest idea is the operating-system framing: the page explains what sits around the hire instead of showing a generic talent marketplace. The two offer cards are structured and easy to compare. The inspected capture had large blank gaps, and the right side of the hero felt underused without a person or product visual.

## Selected direction

Winner: **Staff Domain**.

Its disciplined cobalt-and-lime system, strong type hierarchy, restrained hero, and operational framing are the best fit for FilipinoOutsource.com. The redesign adapts those principles into an original Philippines team planning desk. It does not copy Staff Domain's logo, copy, service names, badges, contour artwork, or card layout.

## Original implementation

- A cobalt rounded hero combines a plain Philippines-specific promise with a locally stored team photo.
- A custom line pattern, warm mango accent, woven `F/O` logo, and cream page background keep the identity distinct.
- The role map covers operations, customer support, admin, and reporting without inventing performance claims.
- The operating-system section uses concrete handoff records, access limits, review ownership, and escalation rules as trust evidence.
- Every primary CTA says `Plan my Philippines team` and leads to `/contact`.
- A routing disclosure near the hero and contact form explains that the site is independent and may route inquiries to a staffing partner that sources only in the Philippines.
- No public pricing, savings claim, testimonial, rating, certification, or competitor asset is used.

## Image notes

The locally stored hero image shows coworkers discussing a planning board around a table. A second image shows coworkers at computers in an office. Neither image is used to claim the subjects' nationality, employer, or profession; nearby copy carries the Philippines-only promise.
